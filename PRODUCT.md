# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Small groups of Steam players who want to find a multiplayer game that fits the whole table. They use the tool while planning what to buy or play together, often with mixed tastes and uneven libraries.

## Product Purpose
The product reads public Steam library and playtime signals, models group taste, and recommends multiplayer games nobody or only part of the group owns. Success means the group trusts the explanation enough to start a shared purchase or play session.

## Brand Personality
Neon command desk, game-native, technically sharp. The interface should feel like a focused multiplayer discovery cockpit rather than a generic SaaS form.

## Anti-references
Avoid plain white dashboards, generic SaaS cards, casino neon overload, purple gradient mush, fake glassmorphism, and decorative sci-fi that gets in the way of reading recommendations.

## Design Principles
- Evidence first: every recommendation should visually connect score, taste match, source marks, and AI reasoning.
- Game-native density: make the page feel built for players without sacrificing scan speed.
- Neon with restraint: use glow and saturated color as state and emphasis, not as wallpaper.
- Trust through structure: keep inputs, status, and result sections predictable even when the visual skin is bold.
- Fast to act: the primary flow is entering IDs, tuning preferences, and comparing cards.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA contrast for all readable text. Respect reduced motion, keep focus states visible, do not rely on color alone for recommendation quality, and avoid flashing or rapid animated effects.
